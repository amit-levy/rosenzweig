Individual and Collective in German-Jewish Life and Culture

The course of emancipation in Germany, and its aftermath over the long 19th Century, offered German Jews opportunities to determine their individual and collective identities in new ways.

Social & Political Life

Exploring communal organization, struggles between orthodox and liberal authorities, and visions of socialist, Zionist, or utopian collectives.

Religious Thought

Posing questions on the importance of individual vs. collective in religious life, including conceptions of revelation and divine address.

Literary Representation

Representations of communal life and inner life depictions (expressionist or stream-of-consciousness) in German-Jewish literature.

Philosophy & Identity

Contributions to Neo-Kantianism, existentialism, and engagement with questions of identity and difference from Enlightenment to 1933.

The Center's interests in the year's theme are broad and interdisciplinary, and we encourage scholars of German-Jewish culture across the humanities to apply.
